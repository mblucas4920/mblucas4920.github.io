5 Criteria for Measuring the Effectiveness of a Performance Management System

Fit with Strategy

A performance management system should aim at achieving employee behavior and attitudes that support the organizationâ€™s strategy, goals, and culture.

Validity

The extent to which a measurement tool actually measures what it is intended to measure

Reliability

The consistency of the results that the performance measure will deliver.

Acceptability

Whether or not a measure is valid and reliable, it must meet the practical standard of being acceptable to the people who use it.

Specific Feedback

A performance measure should specifically tell employees what is expected of them and how they can meet those expectations.
