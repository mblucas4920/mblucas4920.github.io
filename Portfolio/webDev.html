Attached is a link to a website I created for a project in ITEC362 (Introduction to Web Systems) as well as two PDFs containing my initial wire frames sketch for the site, and a mock-UI I designed to base my website off of.

This project is a "fan-site" utilizing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create a stylish and informative website that functions on most standard web browsers. The website is comprised of 15 different webpages, with 4 of them acting as main features and the other 11 being used within an iFrame.

Before creating this website, I had never utilized wireframes or mock-UIs and they both proved to be useful tools. Creating wireframes for my website made me consider the layout of each webpage, as well as the navigation between each page. I designed the mock-UI using Figma and based it off my wireframes. This design became my main reference while coding the CSS for the website. This project taught me the importance of planning.

Working on this project helped me enhance non-technical abilities as it required a great deal of time management, creative thinking, and a willingness to learn, alongside previous HTML knowledge. This valuable experience will help in achieving my post-graduate goal of working with a company that allows me to pursue my interest in website design and development.

About Cola's Restaurant and Internship

Cola's is a "casual" fine dining bar/restaurant located in Columbia, SC that specializes in seafood, steaks, and drinks. I worked as a Website Development Intern at Cola's Restaurant over the Summer of 2025. My duties and responsibilities included designing UI/UX prototypes, creating and optimizing web content for seamless user experience & responsive design, evaluate website design and ensuring it aligned with brand standards/visual identity, and more.

Phase 1: Planning

At the beginning of my internship for Cola’s Restaurant I used Microsoft Project Planner to create a timeline for my project during my internship.

Phase 2: Prototyping

Over the course of my internship, I created both low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

The purpose of the following prototypes was to establish a layout for the website that my supervisor was satisfied with before beginning to design more details.

High-Fidelity Prototypes

The purpose of the following prototypes was to establish a color scheme, and content (text, images, links, etc.), alongside other details such as user interactivity and user accessibility. These high-fidelity prototypes worked as my main reference during the coding process.

Conclusion

My time at Cola’s Restaurant was valuable and rewarding. My design was not made the restaurant’s official webpage during the length of my internship, but my work will be used and referenced in future projects for the company. The biggest difficulties I faced were in the following categories…

Communication. It was difficult at times to communicate with my supervisor due to the nature of his position (he is rarely on-site). This challenge was solved by me beginning to write weekly reports of my work and emailing them to my supervisor.

Responsive design. One of the most significant (technical) challenges I faced was ensuring that the website was responsive on both mobile and desktop devices. I solved this problem with extensive research, trial-and-error, and having access to many screen sizes.

About Capstone Course and Project

UofSC's Capstone course grants students experience through the practical application of project management software, technologies, and practices to the design and implementation of real-world capstone projects.

Our client, Dr. John Gerdes, requested we develop a PHP tutorial website handling both front-end and back-end development. The goal of this project is to provide an interactive learning platform that demonstrates how to use PHP to create and update web forms, as well as generate graphs from user-inputted data. The final product will serve as an educational resource for students learning PHP-based web development

The web enabled database tutorial is built in PHP and hosted via XAMPP. The website exists as “PHP-MySQL-Tutorials” with fully implemented CSS. Another version exists as “PHP-MySQL-Tutorials-minCSS” with minimum CSS intended to only manage the basic layout of the websites content.

The website contains the following webpages...

  1. Index/Landing Page
  2. PHP Form Tutorial Page
  3. PHP MySQL Tutorial(s) Page
  4. W3Schools Tutorial Pages
  5. Inserting Data into a Database Using a Form Page
  6. Updating Data in a Database Using a Form Page
  7. Displaying Data Returned from a Query Page
  8. Data Visualization Page

This project was developed over the course of 8 weeks divided into 4 sprints (each 2 weeks in length).

Sprint 1: Project Charter and Scope of Work (Jufura Media Unlimited)

My group was unique in that we worked with two different clients over the course of 8 weeks. Our first client, Jufura Media Unlimited, took up the entirety of our first sprint. These two weeks were spent communicating with our client to develop a satisfactory project schedule and a reasonable scope of work. Unfortunately, after many lengthy discussions with our client, we came to the conclusion that the requirements being asked of us were not possible for us to achieve with our current skill levels and within the short 8 week time frame. We mutually came to the decisiion to go our separate ways, and our group was then assigned to Dr. John Gerdes, who we spent the remaining 3 sprints working for.

Sprint 2: Low-Fidelity Prototype (Dr. John Gerdes)

Our second sprint was spent communicating with Dr. Gerdes to establish a satsifactory project charter and statement of work. After each of these documents was signed by all group members and by our client, we officially began working on deliverables. During this sprint we designed a low-fidelity prototype (wireframes) to establish the layout of the website and getting it approved before beginning to code.

Sprint 3: ERDs and Alpha-Design Website

Our third sprint was spent researching and writing content for the webpages, an designing entity-relationship diagrams (ERDs) to reference when creating both the front-end and the back-end of the website. The deliverables for this sprint included ERDs and an alpha-design of the website which features a semi-functional MySQL database, and 3 functional webpages.

Sprint 4: Functional Website

Our fourth and final sprint was spent developing CRUD queries and finalizing PHP/CSS code on the remaining webpages. The deliverables for this sprint included a fully functional tutorial website that is capable of successsfully teaching students how to utilize PHP and MySQL together. This website is capable of storing, updating, and displaying user-inputted data.

Conclusion:

The Capstone course and project provided crucial real-world experience in a group setting. This project required a signifigant amount of planning, documentation, communication, collaboration, and coding. In order to make the project more palatable, our group seperated into different roles. I was dubbed the "Development Technical Lead" due to my past in and passion for website development. I was responsible for bridging the gap between our clients goals, and the technical requirements needed by our team to achieve said goals. I compiled our detailed project schedule, and did a majority of the front-end coding (PHP, HTML, CSS).
